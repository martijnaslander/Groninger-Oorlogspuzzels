8 April 2026 (evening)
BP scores recalculated across 3,024 cards — following a sharp observation by Ika van Doorn

Ika van Doorn discovered that the BP scores on multiple cards did not match the description next to them. A card with a quote and an external match sometimes had a lower score than what the BP table on the method page prescribes. This was a bug in the pipeline that generated the scores — the score and the description were calculated independently and had drifted out of sync.

That evening we wrote a recalculation script that walks through every card, queries the checker database (has an external source confirmed this person?) and recalculates the BP score according to exactly the table on the method page. This yielded 204 corrections on the cross-stories page and 2,820 corrections on the archive page.

I deliberately chose to put this entire project online while it is still in development, and to discover through feedback from others what does not work well enough and what can be improved. I think more people should do this — just share what you are working on while you are working on it. Of course you will make mistakes, and as soon as you discover them you fix them. That is the fun of experimenting.

  • 204 BP scores corrected on the cross-stories page
  • 2,820 BP scores corrected on the archive page

  • BP descriptions made consistent with the recalculated scores

Planned improvements

This project is not finished and probably never will be. Below is what we plan to improve, so you know what we are working on and what you can expect.

Saving and sharing stories

Multiple visitors indicated that they want to be able to save or forward certain stories to family members. We are building a way to export stories as a brief summary via a form, so you can save them or email them to someone who might find them useful.

The missing cards

Of the 4,615 cards in the inventory of the Groningen Archives, 3,150 have been transcribed. The remaining cards were deliberately not processed because they contain too little readable text — the pipeline selected the richest cards first, and what remains is generally too thin to transcribe meaningfully. Over 600 cards have no digital scan at all. If anyone discovers that an unprocessed card does contain something valuable, we will gladly process it.

Random sample quality control

The AI transcription has not been independently validated. We want to have at least one hundred cards checked by two independent readers who compare the original scan with our transcription, so we get a reliable picture of the error margin and know where the machine most often goes wrong.
